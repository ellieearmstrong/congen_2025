Instructor

Ellie Armstrong is an Assistant Professor of Evolutionary Genomics at the University of California, Riverside. She previously was a Washington Research Foundation postdoctoral fellow in the lab of Joanna Kelley (UCSC).

She received her PhD at Stanford University with Dmitri Petrov and Liz Hadly, where she worked on the genomics of large carnivores.

Ellie has assembled many reference genomes including the African wild dog, lion, brown bear, Hawaiian tetragnatha, lichens, birds of paradise, gray fox and more.

The Armstrong Lab focuses on a variety of topics spanning from the detection of recent population bottlenecks to uncovering ancient admixture events, with the ultimate goal of understanding how evolutionary processes influence the resilience, adaptation, and long-term viability of species.
