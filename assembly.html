Workshop flow

Below is a suggested flow for the assembly portion of ConGen2025. Adapt the commands and details to your specific dataset and compute environment.

  1. Explore the reads Inspect file names, formats, and coverage estimates. Run quick QC (e.g. FastQC, summary stats) to get a sense of read length and quality.
  2. Run an assembly Choose a basic assembler appropriate for the read type (short-read, long-read, hybrid). Record the parameters you use.
  3. Evaluate the assembly Look at summary stats (N50, total size, number of scaffolds/contigs) and compare to expectations for your species.
  4. Map reads back Align reads to the new assembly to evaluate coverage, potential misassemblies, and regions of poor support.
  5. Interpret and discuss Consider how read type, coverage, and assembler choice affect the outcome, and what this means for downstream conservation genomics analyses.

Terminology

A quick glossary for common assembly and mapping terms used throughout the workshop.

  • Coverage (depth): The average number of reads covering each base in the genome.
  • Contig: A contiguous sequence assembled without gaps.
  • Scaffold: An ordered and oriented set of contigs, often with gaps (Ns) where the precise sequence is unknown.
  • N50: The contig or scaffold length at which 50% of the total assembly length is contained in contigs/scaffolds that length or longer.
  • BUSCO: A method for assessing genome completeness using sets of single-copy orthologs.
